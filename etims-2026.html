eTIMS Rules: The New Reality for Kenyan Businesses

The grace periods are officially over. KRA's automated system validation enforces strict electronic tax invoice rules for individual and corporate tax filings. Ignoring these rules puts your business at immediate risk of audits and heavy penalties.

1. Strict Automated Expense Validation

KRA now automatically matches your declared business expenses against real-time data from TIMS/eTIMS invoices, customs records, and withholding tax filings.

The Golden Rule: If an expense is not supported by a valid eTIMS invoice containing your company PIN, it is automatically disallowed. It will be added back to your net profit and taxed heavily.

2. Who Must On-Board?

Every person or entity running a business in Kenya must be on eTIMS. This includes corporations, sole proprietors, partnerships, and players in the informal sector. Even if your business deals exclusively in VAT-exempt supplies (like schools, hospitals, or specific consulting services), you are still legally required to issue eTIMS invoices.

3. Buying From Small Traders: Reverse Invoicing

How do you claim expenses when buying from casual laborers, smallholder farmers, or informal suppliers who aren't on eTIMS? You must use Buyer-Initiated (Reverse) Invoicing.

  • Log onto the KRA eCitizen portal or dial the USSD code *222#.
  • Generate the invoice on behalf of the supplier using their National ID or mobile number.
  • The supplier will receive an SMS and must approve it via their phone to validate the transaction for your tax records.

4. Survival Checklist for Your Business

  • Enforce a "No eTIMS, No Payment" policy: Never clear a vendor's payment until they furnish you with an eTIMS receipt featuring a valid QR code and control unit number.
  • Verify Invoices: Scan incoming QR codes with your smartphone to verify they link directly to the authentic KRA portal before recording them in your ledger.
  • Reconcile Monthly: Don't wait until tax season. Download your eTIMS Purchase Report from iTax every month and cross-check it against your bank and internal statements.
